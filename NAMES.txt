SUGGESTED PROJECT NAMES
========================

── 1. OrbitForge ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GitHub:  carlok/orbitforge
  Display: OrbitForge

  Reason:
  "Orbit" is mathematically precise — every child primitive in the grammar
  is placed as a G-orbit (Symmetrize_G replicates it across all |G| group
  elements). "Forge" captures the metal additive manufacturing destination
  (DMLS/SLM) and the act of shaping material under constraint. Together they
  compress the two defining ideas — group-orbit geometry and metal fabrication
  — into a single, memorable, POSIX-safe word.

  Tone: technical but strong. Works as a CLI tool name, a paper title prefix,
  and a brand.


── 2. PlatoSDF ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GitHub:  carlok/platosdf
  Display: PlatoSDF

  Reason:
  Directly names the mathematical foundation: the three irreducible finite
  subgroups of O(3) come from the Platonic solids (tetrahedron → Td,
  cube → Oh, icosahedron → Ih). "SDF" signals the representation (Signed
  Distance Function), making the stack immediately legible to anyone in the
  computational geometry or generative design community. No ambiguity about
  what the project does or why it is structured the way it is.

  Tone: academic and precise. Good for a paper repository or research tool.
  Slightly dry as a brand but maximally honest.


── 3. Symmetra ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GitHub:  carlok/symmetra
  Display: Symmetra

  Reason:
  Contraction of "symmetry" + the Latin suffix "-tra" (instrument, tool).
  Evokes the formal group-theoretic core (symmetry groups Td, Oh, Ih are
  the generative engine) without requiring the user to know the mathematics
  upfront. Short, pronounceable, memorable, and domain-agnostic enough to
  cover both the 3D printing application and any future extension to 2D
  rosette groups or other dimensions. No existing prominent open-source
  project uses this name.

  Tone: clean, modern, slightly poetic. Works well as a brand, a library
  name, and a conference demo title.


── RECOMMENDATION ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  For a research/math-first audience:  PlatoSDF
  For a tools/maker/printing audience: OrbitForge
  For general-purpose / future growth:  Symmetra
